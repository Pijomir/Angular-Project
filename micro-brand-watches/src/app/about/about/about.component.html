What are Microbrand Watches?

Microbrand watches are produced in small numbers and offer a change from the mainstream offerings of established manufacturers. Typically more affordable than their well-known counterparts, microbrands often purchase movements from industry suppliers or outsource production entirely. This way they can achieve greater flexibility when it comes to design and pricing, as well as direct distribution between the manufacturer and the customer. Crowdfunding has also become a popular way for microbrands to help bring their projects to life, simultaneously offering enthusiasts a diverse range of designs and materials.

While microbrand watches may lack the heritage of larger brands, they make up for it with their focus on product innovation as well as quality. These watches often feature unconventional designs and materials, catering to enthusiasts looking for something different and unique. Despite their affordability, microbrands typically use movements from reputable suppliers like ETA and Sellita, ensuring their products perform superbly. Although they may not have in-house calibers, microbrands offer customization at lower prices, which makes them an attractive option for those seeking unique timepieces.

With the growing popularity of microbrand watches, the market is bursting with options. From minimalist designs to bold statement pieces, there's something for every taste and style.
